The source

This item contains selected poems from Cardiff Free Library 2.83 (= Cardiff 6). The manuscript is described as follows in the Report on Manuscripts in the Welsh Language, page 104:

Miscellanea, containing 'Historical' and Moral Triads, the Interpretation of Dreams, the 'Histories' of the Dog-star, of Tristan and Esyllt, of the Seven Wise Men of Rome, Religious plays and Dialogues, in verse, between Body and Soul, Arthur and Eliwlod, the king and his servant, the Young Man and the Wren, Eiry Mynydd, selected sayings and passages from 'Dionysius' Cato, Seneca, Marcus Aurelius, etc. Paper; 7¾ x 5¾ inches; 190 pages, stained and imperfect in many places, especially at the corners and margins which have been clumsily covered with tracing paper; circa 1550; bound in morocco.

Unfortunately, several pages have been lost in the meantime, cf. Summary Catalogue of the Manuscripts of South Glamorgan Libraries, Cardiff Central Library, compiled by Graham C.G. Thomas and Daniel Huws (Aberystwyth: National Library of Wales, 1995), page 84:

pp. 130-54 and 163-6 were wanting when the MS was seen in 1978. It was then in a half-leather modern binding by W H Smith.

Among the texts lost are Y brenin ar gwas and the Triads.

Previous editions

Yr Enaid a'r Corff. The text is edited here from Cardiff 6 (c. 1550). It can also be found in Peniarth 65 (end of the sixteenth century). The text has previously been edited in the following previous editions:

  • T.H. Parry-Williams, Canu Rhydd Cynnar (Caerdydd: Gwasg Prifysgol Cymru, 1932), 295-301 (from Cardiff 6)
  • Ifor Williams, ‘Chwarae Ymddiddan yr Enaid a'r Corff’, Bulletin of the Board of Celtic Studies 4 (1929), 36–41 (from Cardiff 6). [another ed. =? Pen. 65 in BBCS iii, 119]
  • Gwenan Jones, Three Welsh religious plays (Bala: R. Evans & Son, The Bala Press, 1939) [University of Minnesota doctoral dissertation, 1918], 238–59 (from Peniarth 65 with variants from Cardiff 6 [apparently unreliable], with English translation).
