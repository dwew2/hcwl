Regular expressions

Regexp (for 'regular expressions') is a search language that allows for very sophisticated searches (including initial mutations). As an example, the various forms of bren(h)in could be found with an expression like this:

            (bvuf)renh?in

(bvuf) means 'one of b or v or u or f';

h? means 'one h or none' (that is, h may be missing).

 

A number of standard concordance software packages (for instance, MonoConc Pro, Concordance) allow regular expressions to be used for searching.
