Testament Newydd 1567: Detholiad o'r darnau a gyfieithwyd gan William Salesbury (TN-Sal)

Testament Newydd ein Arglwydd Jesv Christ. Gwedy ei dynnu, yd y gadei yr ancyfiaith, 'air yn ei gylydd or Groec a'r Llatin, gan newidio ffurf llythyreu y gairiae-dodi. Eb law hyny y mae pop gair a dybiwyt y vot yn andeallus, ai o ran llediaith y 'wlat, ai o ancynefinder y devnydd, wedy ei noti ai eglurhau ar 'ledemyl y tu dalen gydrychiol. (London: by Henry Denham, at the costes and charges of Humfrey Toy, dwelling in Paules church yarde, at the signe of the Helmet, 1567), Mathew 26–8 (tt. 42v–49r), Actau 24–8 (tt. 212v–220v), 2 Corinthiaid 1–9 (tt. 262v–272v)..

Details of the encoding

Verse numbers are not marked in the original. The relevant numbers have been supplied in the markup, within the seg-element, and are displayed in the output files for ease of reference. Direct speech and other quotations are not explicitly marked in the original either, but have been tagged using the q-element in the transcription. For details of the structural markup for chapters and verses, see the notes to the 1588 Bible.

The text includes extensive marginal glosses of a linguistic nature. In the transcription, the word or phrase glossed is marked up using the term-element, and the gloss itself immediately follows, marked up using the gloss-element. Note that although the glosses are placed within the line in the transcription, that are always marginal glosses in the original.

The text uses a second convention effectively as a means of glossing. Parts of words are written in a different font to indicate that the word may be read in its entirety, or with the extra letters omitted. For instance <diernot> may be read as die (that is, diau) or as diernot (that is, diwrnod). In the transcription, the letters marked out in this way are tagged using the hi-element with the rend-attribute "antiqua" (for instance, the example just mentioned is represented as die<hi rend="antiqua">rnot</hi>.

The text contains three special characters: hooked l (for <ll>), eth (ð) (for <dd>) and a double v (vv) (for <w>). These are represented in the TEI-transcription using the character entities &lhook;, &eth; and &vv; respectively. In the diplomatic files, they are represented more or less in the form they are found in the original. In other output files, they are replaced with their modern equivalents.
