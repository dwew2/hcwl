We aim to achieve as equal a distribution of texts both in terms of date and geographical provenance as possible in the Corpus.

 

For this reason, the time-span 1500-1850 has been more or less arbitrarily divided into five 70-year periods (1500-1570, 1570-1640, 1640-1710, 1710-1780, 1780-1850).

 

This is one of the major factors in the selection of specific texts. Of course, actual availability of material often restricts the choice.

 

Where, however, a choice between different texts for a given text type and a period exists, unedited texts are preferred over edited texts, well-preserved texts over fragmentary ones, and texts of the required length (ca 15,000 words) over shorter ones.
