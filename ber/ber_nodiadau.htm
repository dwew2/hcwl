The text

The corpus contains a selection of twenty-four ballads by Ellis Roberts, all published after 1780.

The source

The ballads were transcribed from originals in the National Library of Wales: Cwpwrdd baledi 268, 271, 332, 334, 342, 346, 347, 348, 358, 359, 370, 371, 373, and 374.

The author

Ellis Roberts (d. 1789) was a prolific writer of ballads, religious 'letters' and seven extant interludes. He was a sexton at Llanddoged, Denbighshire, although he may originally have come from further west in Merionethshire.

The language and orthography of the text

The ballads show strong evidence of Ellis Roberts's northern dialect.

Details of the encoding

Bibliographical details of the texts are given in bibl-elements within the sourceDesc-element of the TEI-header. Individual ballads are identified (in the id-attribute of the text-element accompanying the ballad itself, and in the sameAs-attribute of the bibl-element of the bibliographical details) using references derived from John Humphreys Davies's A bibliography of Welsh ballads (London: Honourable Society of Cymmrodorion, 1908-11). These take forms such as "BWB_327-2" where 327 refers to the number of the pamphlet in Davies's listing, and 2 indicates that this is the second ballad within that pamphlet.

Material external to the ballad (names of speakers, tunes, etc.) is not preceded by lb-elements and is not enclosed within l-elements, hence is not assigned a separate line number in any of the output files. The transcriptions generally try to preserve the line arrangements of the originals. However, inconsistencies of line arrangement, usually minor, within a single ballad have been removed. A few prints (for instance, BWB 332(1)) more or less arrange the verse lines as running text. In these cases the line layout has completely been rearranged, and the ballads are presented in the transcription within each logical ballad line rendered as a separate line.

Where stanzas are numbered in the original, these numbers are marked up using the formword element <fw> ... </fw>. Where ballads are dialogues, the speakers identifications are marked with the <speaker> element.

Further details
